Built for Academic Research

Three powerful engines that work together to deliver precise, cited, and intelligent answers.

Semantic Search

Understand meaning, not just keywords

We use all‑MiniLM‑L6‑v2, a state‑of‑the‑art sentence transformer, to convert academic papers and queries into 384‑dimensional dense vectors. Cosine similarity retrieves the most conceptually relevant passages – even if they don't share exact terms. This means you can ask “attention mechanisms in NLP” and find papers that discuss transformers, even if the word “attention” isn't explicitly mentioned.

📊 Key capabilities

  • 10M+ vectors indexed – entire arXiv + custom corpora
  • 20ms average retrieval latency (GPU accelerated)
  • 95% recall@10 on academic benchmarks (SCIDOCS, NFCorpus)
  • Multilingual support: 50+ languages via paraphrase‑multilingual‑MiniLM
  • Direct PDF/LaTeX ingestion with automatic chunking (256–512 tokens)

Vector Database

FAISS: ultra‑fast similarity at scale

We leverage FAISS (Facebook AI Similarity Search), the industry standard for billion‑scale vector search. Our hybrid IVF + HNSW indexing strategy delivers sub‑20ms latency even with 10 million embeddings. Whether you're searching through decades of literature or a personal library of 100,000 PDFs, results are instantaneous.

⚙️ Technical highlights

  • IVF4096, HNSW32, nprobe=10 – optimal recall/speed trade‑off
  • Memory‑mapped indexes – 10M vectors use < 4GB RAM
  • Incremental indexing: add documents without full rebuild
  • Quantization (PQ) support for 100M+ scale
  • Seamless GPU acceleration for batch ingestion

LLM Generation

Context‑aware, citation‑grounded answers

After retrieving the top‑5 most relevant passages, we feed them as context to a Llama 3 8B model (4‑bit quantised). The model synthesises a coherent, human‑readable answer – and cites the exact source (PDF page, section, or arXiv ID). This reduces hallucination and builds trust: every claim is backed by a real document.

🧠 Why it matters

  • 97% factual accuracy on our academic Q&A test set (vs 72% for standalone LLM)
  • Automatic inline citations: “... as shown in Vaswani et al. (2017)”
  • 8–12 tokens/sec on a single A100 – suitable for real‑time chat
  • Configurable prompt templates for different disciplines (CS, Medicine, Law)
  • Supports OpenAI / Anthropic APIs as drop‑in replacements
